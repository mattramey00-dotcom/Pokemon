Strengths & Weaknesses

Know what the deck wants to do — and exactly where it falls apart.

◆ What it's great at

  • Self-powering attacker. Punk Up loads energy from the deck, so Grimmsnarl can attack the turn it evolves.
  • Two-target damage. 180 to the Active plus 30 to a benched Pokémon sets up multi-prize turns.
  • Heals itself. Munkidori shovels your own damage onto the opponent, punishing 2-hit-KO attempts.
  • Hard to one-shot. Stage 2 ex bulk means most non-Grass decks can't KO it in one hit.
  • Strong disruption. Boss's Orders, Iono and Budew let you stall and stunt their setup.
  • Skill-rewarding. In practiced hands it wins almost every game it doesn't brick.

◆ Where it struggles

  • Bricks often. Stage 2 + two support lines = clunky opening hands. Bad hand = bad game.
  • Slow. It's a grind deck. High tie rate — "there is no Game 3" if you stumble early.
  • Grass weakness. Galvantula, Iron Leaves ex, Teal Mask Ogerpon ex one-shot your Grimmsnarl.
  • Aggro pressure. Fast decks (Joltik Box, Raging Bolt) can race you before you set up.
  • Froslass is fragile. It's your only self-damage source — lose it and the engine sputters.
  • Tight bench. Froslass + Munkidori eat bench space, leaving little room for support.
One-line takeaway: Every decision should buy consistency. Mulligan your hands honestly, don't overload your bench into your own Froslass damage, and respect Grass decks.

Matchup Cheat Sheet

Tap a matchup to expand the game plan. Color = how the matchup leans for you.

✕Raging Bolt / Joltik BoxFast + Grass attackers▶
Your worst kind of matchup. Their Grass attackers one-shot Grimmsnarl and they're fast. Plan: race with spread instead of grinding, use Boss's Orders + Counter Catcher to KO their setup pieces early, and lean on Budew/Iono to slow their tempo. You need a clean opener here.
✕Hydrapple exGrass-type, hits your weakness▶
Grass weakness means Grimmsnarl gets KO'd far too easily. Plan: avoid presenting a lone Grimmsnarl; spread heavily with Froslass + Munkidori so you can trade prizes even when your attacker dies. Devolution can blow out their board if they over-evolve.
=Gardevoir exSetup engine, skill matchup▶
Traditionally tough, but very winnable with practice — favored if you out-pilot them. Plan: Iono their setup turns, use Munkidori to keep your board healthy, and use Boss's Orders to snipe their Psychic acceleration before they snowball.
=Charizard exBig single attacker▶
Skill-dependent and close. Plan: they rely on one big attacker — drag up their Pidgeot/setup with Boss's Orders, and use Munkidori healing to survive the 2-hit-KO race. Don't let them get a free turn to build.
✓Gholdengo ex / Ceruledge exCurrently rising decks▶
These are easier for you to handle than the aggro decks they replaced. Plan: play your normal grindy game — spread, heal, swing. Your bulk and Munkidori healing out-value their offense over a long game.
✓Slower / control decksAnything that wants a long game▶
You out-grind grinders. Plan: this is where the 3× Boss's Orders shines — pressure their setup, drag up sitting ducks, and let Froslass + Munkidori inevitably bury them. Patience wins.
Reading the colors: ✓ favored · = skill matchup · ✕ uphill. Even the ✕ games are winnable with a good opener — they just punish bricks hard.

Secrets & Quick Rules

Tech tricks for this deck, plus the core rules a beginner needs at the table.

Deck secrets

Punk Up → Munkidori overflow. Load 4 Darkness onto Grimmsnarl, then Energy Switch one onto Munkidori so it's online to move damage. One ability, two jobs.

TM: Devolution blowout. Spread damage with Froslass/Munkidori, then devolve several of their Pokémon at once to a lower-HP stage — multiple Knock Outs in a single turn.

Defiance Band when behind. You'll usually be down on prizes; if you tech a Defiance Band, Grimmsnarl swings for 210 instead of 180.

Secret Box first, always. It's the engine — fire it ASAP to grab a Supporter + items and dig out of the early game. After it's used, Arven gets much weaker.

Spikemuth Gym = your tutor. It fetches any Marnie's Pokémon, so it pulls every stage of the Grimmsnarl line. Keep one in play.

Protect Froslass. It's your only self-damage source. Shaymin guards it; if it dies before you've banked damage, your engine stalls.

Core rules (beginner)

Going first?
You skip your attack on turn one. Use the turn to flood your bench with Impidimp and Snorunt.
One Energy per turn
From your hand. Punk Up's deck-attachments are extra and don't count.
One Supporter per turn
Arven, Iono, Professor's Research, Boss's Orders all share this limit. Items are unlimited.
Evolving
Can't evolve a Pokémon the turn you played it (Rare Candy & TM: Evolution are the exceptions).
Prizes
Take 1 per Knock Out — but 2 for KOing an ex. Remember the opponent takes 2 when they KO your Grimmsnarl.
Bench limit
Max 5 benched Pokémon. With Froslass + Munkidori eating slots, plan your space.
Win conditions
Take all 6 prizes · opponent has no Pokémon in play · opponent can't draw at start of turn.
Damage counters vs. HP: Froslass places counters (10 each). A Pokémon is Knocked Out when counters meet or exceed its HP — Munkidori just relocates those counters before they finish you off.

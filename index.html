Strengths & Weaknesses

Know what the deck wants to do — and exactly where it falls apart.

◆ What it's great at

  • Self-powering attacker. Punk Up loads energy from the deck, so Grimmsnarl can attack the turn it evolves.
  • Two-target damage. 180 to the Active plus 30 to a benched Pokémon sets up multi-prize turns.
  • Heals itself. Munkidori shovels your own damage onto the opponent, punishing 2-hit-KO attempts.
  • Hard to one-shot. Stage 2 ex bulk means most non-Grass decks can't KO it in one hit.
  • Strong disruption. Team Rocket's Petrel, Judge, Boss's Orders and Budew let you stall and stunt their setup.
  • Skill-rewarding. In practiced hands it wins almost every game it doesn't brick.

◆ Where it struggles

  • Bricks often. Stage 2 + two support lines = clunky opening hands. Bad hand = bad game.
  • Slow. It's a grind deck. High tie rate — "there is no Game 3" if you stumble early.
  • Grass weakness. Grass attackers (e.g. Mega Venusaur) hit Grimmsnarl for double — a real threat.
  • Aggro & Mega pressure. Fast decks and Mega Feraligatr's hitting power can race you before you set up.
  • Froslass is fragile. It's your only self-damage source — lose it and the engine sputters.
  • Tight bench. Froslass + Munkidori eat bench space, leaving little room for support.
One-line takeaway: Every decision should buy consistency. Mulligan your hands honestly, don't overload your bench into your own Froslass damage, and respect Grass decks.

Secrets & Quick Rules

Tech tricks for this deck, plus the core rules a beginner needs at the table.

Deck secrets

Punk Up feeds Munkidori. Punk Up pulls Darkness from your deck onto your Marnie's Pokémon — make sure a Munkidori ends up with Darkness so its Adrena-Brain can move damage that turn.

Run 4 Munkidori for a reason. Multiple Munkidori let you shift big chunks of damage each turn and punish 2-hit-KO attempts by healing yourself — the more in play, the more you move.

Unfair Stamp is your comeback button. As an ACE SPEC you get one — save it to disrupt their hand on a key turn while refilling your own to 5.

Team Rocket's Petrel + Judge. With Iono gone, these are your hand disruption. Use them when the opponent is holding a loaded hand, especially before they combo off.

Spikemuth Gym = your tutor. It fetches any Marnie's Pokémon, so it pulls every stage of the Grimmsnarl line. Keep one in play — you run 4 for consistency.

Protect Froslass. It's your only self-damage source. If it dies before you've banked damage on your own board, your engine stalls — bench it behind something when you can.

Air Balloon vs. deck-out. Against grindy/mill plans, Air Balloon keeps you mobile and helps avoid being decked out in long games.

Core rules (beginner)

Going first?
You skip your attack on turn one. Use the turn to flood your bench with Impidimp and Snorunt.
One Energy per turn
From your hand. Punk Up's deck-attachments are extra and don't count.
One Supporter per turn
Lillie's Determination, Team Rocket's Petrel, Judge, Boss's Orders all share this limit. Items (Poké Pad, Poffin, Rare Candy) are unlimited.
Evolving
Can't evolve a Pokémon the turn you played it (Rare Candy is the exception — it jumps a Basic straight to Stage 2).
Prizes
Take 1 per Knock Out — but 2 for KOing an ex. Remember the opponent takes 2 when they KO your Grimmsnarl.
Bench limit
Max 5 benched Pokémon. With Froslass + Munkidori eating slots, plan your space.
Win conditions
Take all 6 prizes · opponent has no Pokémon in play · opponent can't draw at start of turn.
Damage counters vs. HP: Froslass places counters (10 each). A Pokémon is Knocked Out when counters meet or exceed its HP — Munkidori just relocates those counters before they finish you off.
